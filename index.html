About Me

I am in my final year of BScHons Actuarial Science at Wits with eight ASSA exemptions. Actuarial science pulled me in because the problems are real: the maths underpins decisions about whether people can retire comfortably, access healthcare, or protect their families. That is the kind of work I want to be doing.

My Honours research project puts LSTM and GRU neural networks up against the Lee-Carter model for long-horizon mortality forecasting. The headline result is that a classical model still holds its own in this setting, which is a useful counterpoint to some of the enthusiasm around deep learning in actuarial circles. The finding is not that machine learning does not work; it is that it needs to earn its place.

I am genuinely excited about what AI tools can do for actuaries, and I do not think the profession should be threatened by them. Used well, AI extends what one person can do and frees up time for the kind of judgement that models cannot replicate. I want to be the kind of actuary who knows how to use these tools properly.

Honours Research Project · 2026

Mortality Forecasting: RNNs vs Lee-Carter

Constructed and evaluated Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) and Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU) recurrent neural network architectures for long-horizon mortality forecasting, benchmarked against the Lee-Carter model on a 10-year test set. England and Wales data formed the primary dataset, with Japanese female mortality data sourced from the Human Mortality Database (HMD) providing a key exception case in the analysis.

Key Findings
  • Lee-Carter substantially outperformed both RNN architectures overall
  • RNNs failed to capture mortality improvement trends over the long horizon
  • LSTM outperformed Lee-Carter for Japanese female mortality (Human Mortality Database)
  • GRU generally outperformed LSTM despite having fewer parameters

Personal Project · Financial Modelling

Retirement Savings Analyser

An Excel-based tool for quantifying the impact of retirement product charges on long-run savings outcomes. Built using Discovery's publicly available product data to calculate the reduction in yield, which is the effective drag on investment returns attributable to fees over a savings horizon.
